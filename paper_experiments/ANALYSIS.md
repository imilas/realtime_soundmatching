## Framing

The goal is **comparative**: show **which method suits which kind of loss
landscape, and why the others fail there** — not to maximize every method. The
three synths are landscape archetypes:

| synth | landscape | what it tests |
|---|---|---|
| bandpass_noise | smooth, identifiable | local exploitation |
| am_noise | moderate | balance of explore/exploit |
| add_sinesaw | **flat / non-identifiable** (loss carries ~no info) | robustness to a deceptive surface |

A non-identifiable loss is a *useful stress test*, not a bug to fix. The endgame
is an **intelligent RL agent** that learns priors over which parameter regions
are actually correct (across many targets) and so beats every classical method —
especially on deceptive surfaces where the instantaneous loss can't. The current
tabular QL's failure is the motivating baseline for that agent.

## TL;DR

- **CMA-ES is the best overall method** (best final accuracy and sample
  efficiency on `bandpass_noise` and `am_noise`); **BO is best on the
  multimodal `add_sinesaw`**.
- **BO's huge jumps are real but not a bug-in-outcome.** It is global GP
  exploration: the best point is found *late* (47–74% of trials after eval 100,
  only 2–4% in the initial random phase). It works, but never switches to a
  local exploitation/refinement phase — hence the random-search *look*.
- **`add_sinesaw` is a loss/identifiability problem, not a method problem.**
  At the point of lowest *audio* loss, median *P*-loss is still 0.20–0.37, and
  corr(audio_loss, P_loss) ≈ 0.04–0.34. The optimizers DO visit near-perfect
  points (best P-loss 0.02–0.04) but the loss doesn't reward them. No optimizer
  change fixes this. **Root cause demonstrated below: the SIMSE_Spec loss
  surface is nearly flat — 76% of the parameter space scores within 1.5× of the
  global minimum, and points maximally far from the true params (P-loss 0.79)
  score within 4% of the best.**
- **QL does not learn across trials.** With a persistent Q-table over 5000
  trials, last-20% vs first-20% median P-loss is flat (Δ ≈ +0.001 on bandpass,
  −0.038 on am_noise). The MDP formulation can't localize a target that changes
  every trial and isn't encoded in the state.
- **RandomSearch ignores the objective.** Its best-so-far curve is *identical*
  across all three synths (0.260 / 0.168 / 0.082 / 0.049 at evals 25/50/100/200)
  because it is a seed-determined random *walk*, independent of audio loss.

> Note: `best_p_loss` is the **VISITED** metric — it uses oracle knowledge of

> deploy), which is what matters and tells the comparative story.

## Returned vs Visited — the comparative centerpiece

A deployed method cannot see the true params; it returns the point with the
lowest **audio** loss. "Returned" = P-loss of `argmin(audio_loss)`. "Visited" =
`best_p_loss` (oracle). **gap = returned − visited = how badly the loss deceives
the method.**

| synth | method | returned | visited | gap |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| bandpass_noise | CMA-ES | **0.001** | 0.000 | 0.000 |
| bandpass_noise | HillClimber | 0.012 | 0.007 | 0.005 |
| bandpass_noise | BO | 0.041 | 0.025 | 0.017 |
| am_noise | CMA-ES | **0.008** | 0.004 | 0.005 |
| am_noise | BO | 0.014 | 0.007 | 0.007 |
| am_noise | HillClimber | 0.038 | 0.012 | 0.026 |
| add_sinesaw | **BO** | **0.197** | 0.021 | 0.176 |
| add_sinesaw | RandomSearch | 0.324 | 0.049 | 0.275 |
| add_sinesaw | QL | 0.365 | 0.144 | 0.221 |
| add_sinesaw | CMA-ES | 0.336 | 0.038 | **0.298** |
| add_sinesaw | HillClimber | 0.373 | 0.229 | 0.145 |

**Reading it:**
- **Identifiable landscapes (bandpass, am_noise): gap ≈ 0.** The loss is
  trustworthy, so convergent local methods win outright — **CMA-ES** returns
  0.001 / 0.008 (near-perfect).
- **Flat landscape (add_sinesaw): every method VISITS near-perfect params
  (0.02–0.05) but RETURNS something far off** — the loss can't distinguish them.
  The gap exposes *why*: **CMA-ES has the worst gap (0.298)** despite visiting
  0.038, because it **commits to a single basin** — on a deceptive surface that
  basin is usually wrong. **BO has the best returned (0.197) and a smaller gap
  (0.176)** because global EI exploration **hedges across the flat region**
  instead of committing. The strength of local methods (fast convergence)
  becomes their weakness here.

**Method × landscape suitability (the headline):**

| landscape | best method | why others fail |
|---|---|---|
| smooth, identifiable | CMA-ES (then HillClimber) | global methods waste budget exploring; GD stuck in audio-loss local minima |
| moderate | CMA-ES / BO | GD weak gradients; QL can't localize |
| flat / non-identifiable | BO (least deceived) | convergent methods (CMA-ES, HC) commit to the wrong basin; QL never learned to |

This gap is exactly the opening for the RL agent: learn, across many targets,
which basins are *actually* correct — information no instantaneous loss provides.

## Why `add_sinesaw` + SIMSE_Spec is non-identifiable (demonstrated)

Synth: `process = sineOsc(sine_freq) + sawOsc(saw_freq)`, both freqs in
[20,1000] Hz, scored on magnitude-spectrogram MSE (SIMSE_Spec).

Grid scan for target (saw_freq=200, sine_freq=400), 41×41 over the box:

| rank | saw_freq | sine_freq | audio_loss | P-loss (0=correct) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 265 | 388 | 0.03976 | 0.068 |
| 2 | 94 | 314 | 0.03993 | 0.140 |
| 3 | 755 | 559 | 0.04037 | **0.589** |
| 8 | 926 | 142 | 0.04137 | **0.787** |

**1280 of 1681 grid points (76%) have audio_loss within 1.5× of the global
minimum.** Points at the opposite corner of parameter space (P-loss 0.79) are
within 4% of the best loss. The loss surface is almost flat — it carries almost
no information about the true parameters.

Mechanism:
1. A **sawtooth fills the spectrum with a dense harmonic comb** (harmonics every
   `saw_freq` Hz to Nyquist). Different saw freqs produce broadband combs that
   overlap heavily in a magnitude spectrogram.
2. The **sine is a single peak among dozens of saw harmonics**, so moving
   `sine_freq` changes one bin's energy — negligible in an MSE over the whole
   spectrogram.
3. **Magnitude + compression (`clip_spec`)** squashes dynamic range, making
   distinct spectra look alike.

Implication: the fix is a **more discriminative loss/representation**, not a
better optimizer. Note `config.SYNTH_LOSS` assigns `add_sinesaw → SIMSE_Spec`,
but GD's `SYNTH_LOSS_MAP` assigns sine-based synths → **JTFS** (joint
time-frequency scattering), which is far more sensitive to exact tonal content.
The config comment claims "JTFS excluded ... spectral shape captures tonal
sine+saw content adequately" — the data shows it does **not**. Candidate fixes:
JTFS, a peak-location-aware loss, or a per-component (pitch-aware) loss.

## Per-method assessment & best-practice issues

### CMA-ES — best overall, mostly sound
- Operates in normalized [0,1]^2, sigma0=0.3, library defaults otherwise.
- Failure rate climbs on harder problems: %trials with best_p_loss>0.2 is
  4% (bandpass) → 20% (am_noise) → 67% (add_sinesaw, but that's the loss).
- **Improvement:** restarts (IPOP/BIPOP-CMA-ES) to escape local optima on
  am_noise; the `cma` library supports `restarts`.

### BO — strong best-found, no exploitation phase
- `skopt.Optimizer(base_estimator="GP", acq_func="EI", n_initial_points=10)`.
- skopt's default GP **estimates Gaussian observation noise**; on a
  near-deterministic objective this inflates posterior uncertainty and keeps EI
  exploring globally forever (explains the √2 jumps).
- **Improvement (test first):** set GP `noise` ≈ 1e-10 (near-deterministic);
  optionally lower EI `xi` to exploit; consider a short local-refinement phase
  on the incumbent at the end. Also: GP at 200 points is O(n³) slow.

### HillClimber — good median, gets stuck
- Fixed σ=0.05, greedy accept, no restart. mean ≫ median (0.12 vs 0.012 on
  bandpass); %stuck (>0.2) = 15% / 44% / 71%.
- **Improvement:** random restarts on stagnation; adaptive step size (1/5
  success rule) — both standard and cheap.

### RandomSearch — not a standard baseline
- It is a Gaussian random *walk* (σ=0.1) that resets to the last point each
  step and never uses the loss. Hence synth-independent best-so-far.
- **Improvement:** replace with canonical **uniform** random sampling over the
  box (the standard "random search" floor). A σ=0.1 walk actually explores
  *less* than uniform sampling, so it isn't even a valid lower bound.

### GD — weak on am_noise / add_sinesaw (now n=200, confirmed)
- RMSProp, lr=0.045, grad-clip 1.0, 200 steps, audio-loss gradients (SIMSE_Spec
  / DTW_Envelope / JTFS). Step sizes tiny (~0.02).
- On am_noise it barely moves (0.386→0.376 over 200 steps) — weak gradient
  signal from DTW_Envelope, or lr too low. Returned P-loss is among the **worst**
  of any method on am_noise (0.488) and add_sinesaw (0.520).
- Completed to **n=200** (2026-05-30); the weakness is not a small-n artifact.
- Also the most expensive method on bandpass: ~432 s/trial, ~2.1 s/eval (real
  per-step cost, not compile — see efficiency analysis), vs ~23 ms for a
  black-box render.
- **Improvement:** multi-start GD; per-loss lr tuning; check gradient norms are
  non-trivial for DTW/JTFS before/after clipping; drop the redundant param-
  extraction forward pass (~halves per-step cost).

### QL — does not learn; MDP mismatch
- State = (position bins, loss bucket); actions = 3^d grid steps; reward = Δloss.
- The target changes every trial and is **not** in the state, so no stationary
  policy can point toward it. The agent can only learn a weak "keep moving if
  improving" heuristic. Last-20% vs first-20% performance is flat over 5000
  trials → the persistent Q-table adds nothing.
- **Improvement (research-level):** encode target-relative information in the
  state (e.g. recent loss gradient / direction), or reframe as contextual
  bandit / learned policy with the target spectrum as context. As a plain
  tabular grid-walker it cannot win.

## v2 agent A/B (preliminary, matched seeds)

Candidate fixes vs baselines on rendered audio (scratch only, not canonical
pkls). Internally matched seeds; small n, so read directionally.

**BO v2** (GP noise=1e-10 + local refinement), n=12, budget=80, median best_p:
- bandpass: 0.061 → **0.041**; am_noise: 0.096 → **0.038** (wins 10/12). Clear win.

**HillClimber v2** (restarts + 1/5-rule adaptive step), n=30, budget=200:
- bandpass: median 0.023 → **0.012**, mean 0.203 → **0.134** (tail cut). Clear win.
- am_noise: median 0.231 → 0.249 (slightly worse), mean 0.319 → **0.272**, wins
  17/30. Mixed — restarts help the failure tail, adaptive step is a wash on median.

Both v2 agents also pass a synthetic convex-objective check (HC v2 0.0069 vs base
0.0178; BO v2 0.0052). Run full-n A/B (E2) before any paper claim.

## Rendering is deterministic (no stochastic-loss confound)

Verified: rendering the same params twice gives **identical** audio (self-loss
0.0) for all three synths, including the noise-based ones (Faust seeds its
noise). So the loss is deterministic; cross-method variance is from targets/seeds
and optimizer behavior, not render noise. (The HC am_noise bench median 0.231 vs
canonical 0.038 — with matching *means* 0.319/0.288 — is small-n seed sampling,
not noise: motivates E1's matched n.)

## E3 — controlled wall-clock (2026-05-31)

Re-measured all methods **interleaved** (back-to-back per repeat → shared
background load, removing the confound that the canonical `duration_s` were
recorded at different times). Per-eval cost isolated from fixed setup/JIT via a
two-budget slope (B1=5, B2=55, 3 reps). Reproduce: `bench_walltime.py`.

| synth | method | ms/eval | reach% (P≤0.05) | evals→thr | sec→thr |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| bandpass | RandomSearch | 22 | 52% | 70 | 1.5 |
| bandpass | HillClimber | 22 | 81% | 39 | 0.9 |
| bandpass | **CMA-ES** | 25 | **96%** | 42 | **1.0** |
| bandpass | BO | 208 | 88% | 43 | 8.9 |
| bandpass | GD | **2212** | 56% | 13 | **28.8** |
| am_noise | CMA-ES | 286 | 80% | 33 | 9.4 |
| am_noise | BO | 564 | 91% | 48 | 26.8 |
| am_noise | GD | 55 | **14%** | 14 | 0.8* |
| add_sinesaw | CMA-ES | 19 | 56% | 37 | 0.7 |
| add_sinesaw | BO | 231 | 96% | 60 | 13.9 |
| add_sinesaw | GD | 24 | **6%** | 10 | 0.3* |

**Read `sec→thr` ONLY with `reach%`.** It averages over trials that reach the
threshold, so a weak method looks misleadingly fast (* GD am_noise 0.8 s and
add_sinesaw 0.3 s are over the **14% / 6%** of trials that ever reach 0.05 — GD
mostly *fails* there; the fast time is a lucky minority, not efficiency).

**What holds:**
1. **GD per-eval cost is wildly synth-dependent:** 2212 ms (bandpass) vs 55 ms
   (am_noise) vs 24 ms (add_sinesaw). The differentiable noise+filter graph for
   bandpass is ~90× the oscillator synths.
2. **On identifiable bandpass, gradient-free wins outright:** CMA-ES reaches the
   target in 96% of trials at 1.0 s; GD reaches it in only 56% and is **~29×
   slower wall-clock (28.8 s)** despite needing fewer iterations.
3. **am_noise is loss-bound, not method-bound:** all gradient-free methods sit at
   ~283 ms/eval because the numpy DTW_Envelope loss dominates (a fixable
   inefficiency — reuse the JAX DTW for evaluation). GD's cheap JAX DTW (55 ms)
   doesn't help it because it only solves 14% of targets.

**Caveats:** BO's per-eval (GP refit O(n³)) is **not** constant, so the 5→55
slope *underestimates* its cost at budget 200 — re-measure BO with B2≈200 before
quoting. add_sinesaw `sec→thr` is noisy (non-identifiable; most methods rarely
reach 0.05) — use the returned-vs-visited framing there, not wall-clock.

## Caveats for the write-up (fairness)

1. **Unequal n — mostly resolved.** GD/HC/RS/CMA/BO are now all n=200 with
   matched seeds (trial index = seed). Only QL remains off-protocol (trained,
   n=thousands) — keep it separate when ranking.
2. **Budget.** These pkls are 200 evals; `config.EVAL_BUDGET = 400`. State which
   the paper uses and regenerate consistently.
3. **QL is "trained," others are not.** QL carries a Q-table across trials; the
   gradient-free methods start cold each trial. Different protocol — report it
